# Fermat-quotient formula for full predecessors

Write

```text
X=M*P,
M=15099,
P=6911089648497401,
O=ord_X(2)=1860810887857924950.
```

The prime `P` satisfies

```text
ord_P(2)=(P-1)/8.
```

For an integer `z` coprime to `P`, define the Fermat quotient

```text
Q_P(z) = (z^(P-1)-1)/P (mod P).
```

## 1. Exact predecessor formula

Let `n` be a full output and let `s` be its least full label:

```text
2^s*n == 1 (mod X).
```

For a full-order layer `q>=0`, the corresponding predecessor is

```text
m_q=(2^(s+qO)*n-1)/X.
```

Then exactly

```text
m_q == -M^(-1)*(Q_P(n)+(s+qO)*Q_P(2)) (mod P).    (1)
```

### Proof

Put

```text
y=2^(s+qO)*n=1+P*t.
```

The standard Fermat-quotient product law gives

```text
Q_P(y)==Q_P(n)+(s+qO)*Q_P(2) (mod P).
```

On the other hand,

```text
(1+P*t)^(P-1)==1-P*t (mod P^2),
```

so

```text
Q_P(y)==-t (mod P).
```

Since `m_q=t/M`, formula (1) follows.

## 2. Explicit constants

The exact certificate obtains

```text
Q_P(2)=16863502182285.
```

It is nonzero modulo `P`.  Therefore the predecessor residues form a genuine
arithmetic progression in `q`.  Its increment modulo `P` is

```text
-M^(-1)*O*Q_P(2)
 == 6336381663004171 (mod P).
```

This agrees with the `P`-component of the previously certified full
predecessor step

```text
63726582940809041391 (mod X).
```

## 3. Zero-layer criterion

The `q=0` predecessor is a full output precisely when both of the following
hold:

1. its component modulo `M` belongs to the `2154` inverse-power classes;
2. the value

```text
-M^(-1)*(Q_P(n)+s*Q_P(2)) (mod P)
```

belongs to the subgroup generated by `2 modulo P`.

Thus the unresolved distribution of zero-layer targets is an exact joint
problem involving:

- the discrete logarithm `s` of `n^(-1)` in the base-2 subgroup;
- the Fermat quotient `Q_P(n)`;
- membership of one affine combination in the same index-eight subgroup.

## 4. Significance

Formula (1) replaces repeated exponentiation modulo `P^2` by an affine
Fermat-quotient condition.  It supplies the correct bridge to character-sum or
discrepancy estimates for the forced populations of zero-layer targets.

Known general estimates for consecutive Fermat quotients are not immediately a
numerical certificate at the present cutoffs: their strongest individual-prime
forms become nontrivial only at lengths around `P^(1/2+epsilon)` and retain
implicit asymptotic constants.  The project therefore needs a specialised
bound for the subgroup-restricted affine expression in (1), or an exact finite
certificate exploiting the smooth subgroup order.
